Manchester City FC: A Brief History

Manchester City was founded in 1880 as St. Mark's (West Gorton) Football Club. The club went through several name changes before settling on Manchester City in 1894. They initially played at several different grounds before moving to their current home, the Etihad Stadium.

Recent Success

Manchester City enjoyed periods of success in the early 20th century. They won their first league title in the 1936-1937 season and followed it up with an FA Cup win in 1956. However, the club went through years of decline, and by the late 1990s, they were playing in the lower divisions of English football.

Manchester City's ongoing goal is likely to maintain their success in the English Premier League. This includes aiming for more league titles, achieving consistent top-four finishes, and competing for domestic cup competitions like the FA Cup and the EFL Cup.
